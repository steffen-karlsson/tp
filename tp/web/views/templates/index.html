About this project


The purpose of this project, is to improve the quality of the rating system of rating site Trustpilot, by providing differentiated scores for different categories, which make up what we "feel" determine the quality of a webshop.
The project was made for the course "02819 Data Mining using Python" at DTU (Technical University of Denmark).

Why is this necessary?
The current system is limited, by the fact that most reviews do not include all factors, often only about price or delivery.
This compromises the rating system, because it might not correctly show how good a store is at handling RMA.

What are the categories, you judge the quality of a webshop by?
At the moment: RMA, price and delivery, in addition to this we also have a general "rating", which is comprised of all the text in a review.
The improvement is made through, the use of sentiment analysis, statistical classification and datamining.

What is the process of the system?
The process is simple, first individual reviews are downloaded from the website.
Next our system reads the reviews, which combined with statistical classification and sentiment analysis, allows us to determine what is being talked about in the review and the opinion of the person reviewing the webshop.

Why is the userinterface implemented as a Greasemonkey script?
By using a Greasemonkey script, we are able to augment rather than replace the website, which we feel is the best solution, since Trustpilot is already an awesome page.

What technologies do you use?
The system is written in Python, using the library NLTK among others.

Do you intend to release the source code?
At the moment, no.
However we might release the HTML scraper framework we created for this project.
